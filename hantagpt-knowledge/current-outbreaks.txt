CURRENT OUTBREAK INTELLIGENCE — MV HONDIUS HANTAVIRUS CLUSTER
Last updated: 7 May 2026

A multi-country hantavirus cluster has been linked to travel on the MV Hondius cruise ship. Public health agencies including WHO, ECDC, CDC, UKHSA, and national authorities are monitoring exposed passengers and contacts.

As of 6 May 2026, ECDC reported seven cases in the cluster, including three deaths, one critically ill case, two symptomatic cases, and one case with unknown clinical status. WHO and media reports around 7 May 2026 described up to eight linked or suspected cases.

The outbreak is suspected to involve Andes virus, a hantavirus strain associated with Argentina and Chile. Andes virus is notable because, unlike most hantaviruses, limited person-to-person transmission has been documented, usually involving prolonged close contact.

Authorities have advised some exposed passengers or contacts to self-isolate or remain under observation. Available public reporting does not provide a single reliable global count of all people currently quarantined or monitored.

Known fatalities reported in connection with the cluster include three deaths: a Dutch couple and a German national. Additional passengers and contacts are being monitored internationally.

Risk to the wider public has been assessed as low by European public health authorities, but investigation and contact tracing remain ongoing.
