CURRENT OUTBREAK INTELLIGENCE — MV HONDIUS HANTAVIRUS CLUSTER
Last updated: 7 May 2026

A multinational hantavirus cluster investigation is ongoing following exposure linked to the Dutch cruise vessel MV Hondius, operated by Oceanwide Expeditions.

The vessel departed Ushuaia, Argentina on 1 April 2026 and travelled through parts of South America and the South Atlantic before heading toward Africa and Europe. Public health agencies including the World Health Organization (WHO), ECDC, UKHSA, CDC, Dutch authorities, Singaporean authorities, and South Atlantic territorial health agencies are participating in contact tracing and monitoring operations.

As of 7 May 2026:

* WHO reported five confirmed hantavirus cases out of eight suspected cases connected to the outbreak.
* Multiple fatalities have been reported, including a Dutch couple and a German national, although not all fatalities have been laboratory-confirmed as hantavirus infections.
* Additional symptomatic individuals remain under medical observation.

The outbreak is suspected to involve Andes virus, a hantavirus strain found in parts of South America that is notable for limited documented person-to-person transmission under prolonged close-contact conditions.

Passengers and crew from at least twelve nationalities were linked to the vessel. Individuals disembarked at St Helena in the South Atlantic on 24 April before travelling internationally to countries including the Netherlands, United Kingdom, United States, Singapore, Germany, Switzerland, Canada, and others.

Public health agencies in multiple countries are monitoring exposed passengers, airline contacts, healthcare contacts, and close contacts. Some exposed individuals have been advised to self-isolate or remain under observation due to the hantavirus incubation period, which may extend up to six weeks.

Two individuals in Singapore linked to the cruise were placed in isolation and tested for hantavirus after exposure during shared travel routes involving South Africa.

Authorities in US states including Georgia and Arizona confirmed monitoring of passengers returning from the cruise, although no symptoms had been reported among those individuals at the time of reporting.

The United Kingdom Health Security Agency and territorial authorities associated with St Helena and Tristan da Cunha initiated contact tracing and precautionary self-isolation guidance for potentially exposed individuals.

The WHO assessed the overall public health risk as low, although investigation and monitoring activities remain ongoing.

The outbreak drew significant international attention due to:

* suspected Andes virus involvement
* multinational exposure routes
* maritime transmission concerns
* extended incubation periods
* rare concerns regarding possible person-to-person transmission

Argentinian health authorities announced plans to test rodent populations in Ushuaia following the outbreak investigation.

The MV Hondius is expected to continue toward the Canary Islands while authorities coordinate ongoing health measures, monitoring, and screening procedures.
